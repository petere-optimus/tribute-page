Dr Nnamdi Azikiwe

Father of Nigerian Nationalism

Dr Nnamdi Azikiwe mounting a guard of honour while in uniform as Nigeria's President and Commander-in-Chief of Nigeria's Armed Forces

Here is a timeline of the key events in Dr Azikiwe's life

  • 1904 - Born in Zungeru, Niger state, Nigeria
  • 1920 - Attended Hope Waddell Training College, Calabar, Cross River state, Nigeria
  • 1925 - Graduated top of his class from Methodist Boys' High School Lagos
  • 1926 - Graduated from Storer College
  • 1930 - Graduated from Lincoln University, Pennsylvania
  • 1934 - Received Masters degree in Anthropology from University of Pennsylvania
  • 1934 - Became the editor-in-chief at the African Morning Post in Ghana
  • 1937 - Founded the West African Pilot in Lagos
  • 1944 - Co-founded the National Council of Nigeria and the Cameroons (NCNC) alongside Herbert Macaulay
  • 1946-1960 - Served as secretary-general and president of NCNC
  • 1947 - Became Member of Nigerian Legislative council
  • 1953 - Elected to Eastern Region Assembly of Nigeria
  • 1954 - Became Premier of Eastern Region of Nigeria
  • 1959 - Became President of the Nigerian Senate
  • 1960 - Became 1st indigenous governor-general of independent Nigeria
  • 1963-1966 - Served as President of Nigerian republic
